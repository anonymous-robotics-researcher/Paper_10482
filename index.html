Evaluation of Retargeting Methods

The video below shows the example of retargeting results using different retargeting methods. Mink[1] shows unnatural locomotion patterns where the humanoid appears to walk on a tightrope, and SINK[2] shows more human-like results, but still shows some floating and penetration. Our PhySINK shows not only remaining human-like, but also physically reliable motions.

[1] Kevin Zakka. Mink: Python inverse kinematics based on MuJoCo, 2025.
[2] He et al. Learning human-to-humanoid real-time whole-body teleoperation, IROS 2024.

Qualitative Comparison with GMR

The video below shows the qualitative comparison with GMR[3]. GMR retargets motion using a fixed height derived from the first component of beta, which is the maximum parameter that determines the shape of human motion. However, this parameter may not correspond to the actual height of the human. As a result, GMR exhibits imperfect retargeting when the human subject is significantly smaller or larger than average.

Imitation Performance

In motion imitation tasks, we train policies on PHUMA and AMASS, and evaluate them on unseen motions. We use MaskedMimic[4] for training, and all policies are trained on IsaacGym. The videos below show qualitative motion imitation results using the student policy on unseen motions. The ghost represents the reference motion to be imitated. As shown, the policy trained on PHUMA demonstrates better motion imitation performance compared to the policy trained on AMASS.

[4] Tessler et al, MaskedMimic: Unified Physics-Based Character Control Through Masked Motion, SIGGRAPH 2024.

Path Following Performance

In path following tasks, we distill the teacher policy by providing only the pelvis information (position and rotation) from the reference motions. We train path following policies on PHUMA and AMASS, and evaluate them on unseen paths. The videos below show qualitative path following results using the student policy on unseen paths.

Imitation Performance

In motion imitation tasks, we train a policy on PHUMA and evaluate it on unseen motions from test videos, comparing it with a policy trained on AMASS. We use MLP version of kungfubot[5] for training. All policies are trained on Isaacgym. The video below shows qualitative motion imitation results using the student policy on unseen motions and test videos. The ghost represents the reference motion to be imitated. As shown, the student policy trained on PHUMA demonstrates better motion imitation performance compared to the policy trained on AMASS.
